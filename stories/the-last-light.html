The Last Light

The lighthouse keeper watched the sun dip below the horizon for what she believed would be the last time.

She pressed the ignition switch out of habit. The great lamp above sputtered, caught, and began its slow rotation.

II

The radio had gone silent three weeks ago. Messages thinned from hourly bulletins to nothing at all.

She stayed disciplined, measuring oil and keeping logs like a clock that refused to stop.

III

On the fourth week, a small wooden boat appeared. A boy stepped onto the dock and asked, "Is the light still on?"

"Good," he said, "People are going to need it."
