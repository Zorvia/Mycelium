Beneath the Waves

The city had no name that human mouths could shape. Its spires rose from the ocean floor like fingers of a buried giant.

Maren adjusted her regulator and followed coordinates from her grandmother's journal.

The Descent

At nearly four hundred meters, the suit creaked under pressure as she passed columns carved with impossible creatures.

Then she saw it: a real doorway with hinges, open in the deep.

What She Found

Beyond that door, a dry room waited with shelves of intact books. In the center, one open volume showed an illustration of Maren entering the very room.

She sat down and began to read.
